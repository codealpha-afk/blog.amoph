The contemporary work of the Ancient Martinist Order in the Philippines (AMOPH) formally began around 2015. Initial contact was established through online correspondence with Sar HDO AGLA in Perth, through which the first transmission into the Martinist tradition was received by a Filipino aspirant. The first initiation was conducted in 2016 in Cebu, Philippines.

From this beginning, the Work was established locally. The transmission provided the necessary foundation for the Order’s presence in the Philippines, allowing its teachings and initiatic practices to take root and be carried forward in accordance with Martinist principles.

In the years that followed, the Work developed through gatherings and retreats held within the country. These retreats have been conducted annually in the Philippines, supporting both the continuity of the tradition and its steady development within the local context.

As the Work matured, members of AMOPH also participated in initiatic activities beyond the Philippines, taking part in Work conducted in other countries in the region. This reflects the Order’s connection to the wider current of Martinism in Southeast Asia.

This account records the beginnings and development of AMOPH as one expression of the Martinist tradition in the Philippines. It does not attempt to encompass the full historical or contemporary presence of Martinism in the country.

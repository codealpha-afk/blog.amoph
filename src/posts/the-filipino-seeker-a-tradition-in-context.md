There is a quality of the genuine esoteric tradition that resists easy announcement. The true Order never advertises itself, for such action would not achieve its purpose, which is Initiation. Initiation cannot be bought — but it can be transmitted personally by those in the direct chain of authority to students who are properly prepared. It is in this spirit that the Martinist current arrived in the Philippines — not as a public movement, but as a quiet transmission between those who were ready to receive it.

---

## What Martinism Is — And What It Is Not

Before speaking of the tradition in the Philippine context, it is worth being precise about what Martinism actually is, since the name is often misunderstood or conflated with other currents.

Martinism as we know it today can be divided into three forms through which it has been chronologically transmitted: the theurgic tradition of Martinez de Pasqually, the Masonic Templarism of Jean-Baptiste Willermoz, and the Christian Theosophy of Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin.  Of these three streams, it is the third — Saint-Martin's silent Way of the Heart — that forms the living core of what the Ancient Martinist Order Philippines transmits.

The Martinism of Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin is a mystical tradition in which emphasis is placed on meditation and inner spiritual alchemy. Saint-Martin explained it as a silent "way of the heart" to attain reintegration.This is not a philosophy to be debated. It is a path to be walked — inwardly, quietly, and with full sincerity.

---

## The Chain of Transmission

The Martinist tradition does not begin with a book or a syllabus. It begins with a person — and with the moment one person transmits something living to another. Our Order preserves and transfers the Martinist Order's initiatic path and teachings, which was founded in the 17th century by Martinez de Pasqually and propagated by Louis-Claude de Saint-Martin in the 18th century, and reawakened in the 19th century by Doctor Gerard Encausse and Robert Ambelain. 

This chain did not stop in Europe. The AMO Philippines continues the esoteric tradition of Russian Martinism by Nikolay Novikov, Grigory Mebes, Vladimir Shmakov, and Valentin Tomberg, from Bishop Elias Ibrahim's transmission to the Venerable Wise Master. The Philippine chapter of this tradition thus carries within it not one lineage but several — French, Russian, and Australian — converging into a single unbroken current.

The AMO Philippines was founded by six chosen brethren from different places around the Philippines and is under the mentorship of the Venerable Wise Master of A.G.L.A in Perth, Australia. The AMO Philippines has unbroken lines of initiatic transmission since time immemorial. It does not conduct so-called "Astral Initiation or Self-Initiation" — it offers a practical form of transmission through physical temple initiation.

## The Three Degrees and the Work of Reintegration

The Order consists of simple adherents and of Initiates divided into three grades — two probationary degrees and one grade. This grade, the S∴I∴ or "Unknown Superior," is only granted to members who show themselves worthy by their behaviour in their daily life, as well as by their special knowledge concerning the doctrine and workings of the Passed Masters of the tradition. 

The goal toward which all three degrees orient the seeker is the same: reintegration. Martinism teaches that humanity in its original state was a divine being, having fallen from this divine state due to lust for personal power and materialism. The Martinist's desire is to return to that divine state through reintegration with the Divine. 

This is not a process that can be rushed or purchased. True esoterism cannot be sold. It is conquered — slowly, in a process in which each person develops his own true Self, in order to achieve a higher comprehension of the reality of the world that surrounds us.

## Who May Enter

The tradition has never been for the few in the sense of the privileged. It has always been for the few in the sense of the sincere. The AMO Philippines is open to all persons over the age of 21, without distinction of gender, race, or religious affiliation, provided they are of good moral character, law-abiding, and affirm a belief in a Supreme Being. The initiation is transmitted to every sincere seeker for free. 

Martinism does not adhere to any exclusive religious confession, but insists on an absolute respect for all of humanity and our individual quest for the sacred — the light of Truth that shines behind all religions. The Filipino seeker, whatever their background, need not abandon any prior commitment to walk this path. They need only arrive with sincerity and the genuine desire to know what lies beneath the surface of things.

## A Living Current in the Archipelago

The Martinist tradition did not arrive in the Philippines to replace what was already here. It arrived to deepen it — to offer, to those who had already passed through the outer courts of moral and fraternal formation, what might be called the inner chamber of the same temple.

For centuries, the visible and invisible schools of mysteries had the privilege and the power to access an ancient source of knowledge. If the candidate proved his true interior and external force, his true will and his strongest perseverance, he would be received among the enlightened ones. This same threshold now exists in the Philippines — not as a memory of something European, but as a living reality transmitted by Filipinos to Filipinos, in the same unbroken chain that stretches back to the mystics of 18th century France.

The hour, as the Order itself has said, has arrived. There is much to be done and little time to do it. The time is now — not yesterday, and certainly not tomorrow.
